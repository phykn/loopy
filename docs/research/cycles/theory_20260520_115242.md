# Loopy Theory Cycle 20260520_115242

## Target

Make the Loopy core theory simpler without losing the rejection boundary.

## Claim

`Loopy = Loop(Unstable -> Verified -> Stable)` is simple enough.

## Evidence

- The formula is short.
- It captures the current distinction between rough work and final theory.
- It avoids the earlier mistake of forcing a preferred conclusion to become true.

## Objection

`Verified` can sound like approval after the fact. The important boundary is harsher: the check must be able to reject the work.

If rejection is hidden, Loopy can degrade into polishing or confirming what it already wanted.

## Revision

Use `Tested` instead of `Verified`.

```text
Loopy = Loop(Unstable -> Tested -> Stable)
```

The test must be able to reject the work.

## Why It Is Stronger

The revision is just as short, but it makes the rejection boundary visible.

It also reads less like certification and more like a loop that can fail.

## Progress Toward Target

This improves the core formula without adding complexity.

The change is stable enough to promote because it preserves the minimum theory and makes the boundary clearer.

Can the `loopy-theory` skill be simplified to match this formula directly?
